The buildings at the Huntsville Botanical Garden feature a range of architectural styles from stately Southern Colonial to rustic, open-air structures designed to blend with the natural environment. 
1 — Propst Guest Center: This facility is designed in a stately four-column Southern Colonial style, offering a sense of grandeur to guests upon arrival. It is a modern, two-story structure with an exterior featuring a structural steel frame, brick, stone, and cast stone elements. Within the center is a Glass Conservatory with classical architecture and a unique curvilinear glass roof, giving it a faceted, jewel-like appearance that harmonizes with the neoclassical visitor center.
2 — Teledyne Terrace: An outdoor viewing and event area that serves as an extension of the guest center facilities.
5 — Murray Hall: This venue features an elegant, neutral-toned interior with expansive windows on three sides that let in natural light and offer sweeping views of Little Smith Lake. It has a wraparound deck that enhances the indoor-outdoor flow.
10 — Summer House: A charming structure located within the garden grounds, its architecture is simple and designed for guests to rest and enjoy the surrounding natural beauty.
23 — Anderson Education Center & Purdy Butterfly House: The Purdy Butterfly House within the center is a large, unique open-air, screened structure that creates a balanced ecosystem for butterflies and other small wildlife. The design ensures an immersive experience while still being connected to the outdoors.
25 — Boeing-Toyota Amphitheatre: This is an open-air amphitheater with semi-circular seating surrounding a stage. It incorporates a touch of historical grandeur by using Classical Doric columns salvaged from the demolished 1914 Madison County Courthouse to frame the stage.
26 — Linda J. Smith Administration Building: A modern office building providing functional space for administrative operations.
27 — Nichols Arbor: This venue blends rustic tradition with elegance, featuring exposed wooden doors, chandeliers, and a library. The space has a distinct garden-inspired theme with trellises set into the walls, complemented by an adjacent courtyard with a pergola and chandelier.
28 — Grisham Pavilion: A large, covered outdoor pavilion with a practical design, ideal for hosting community picnics and open-air events.
Carriage House: Located within the guest center complex, this building exudes rustic sophistication with its high-beamed ceilings adorned with bistro lights, elegant chandeliers, and large carriage doors that open to incorporate the garden views.
