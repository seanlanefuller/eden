The Huntsville Botanical Garden categorizes its diverse plant life into two primary collections: 
1. Botanical Collection
This collection focuses on plant species and hybrids specifically suited for the North Alabama region. Major groups include: 
Trilliums: A flagship collection featuring over 30 species native to the Tennessee Valley and nearly 200 selected cultivars.
Azaleas & Rhododendrons: Over 3,000 specimens are featured, particularly along the Bush Azalea Trail, including many species native to the southeastern U.S..
Native Trees & Shrubs: Significant plantings of dogwoods, magnolias, native hydrangeas, camellias, and hollies.
Ferns: The Fern Glade showcases approximately 150 species of deciduous and evergreen ferns. 
2. Display Collection
These plants are arranged in themed gardens for aesthetic and educational purposes: 
Herbs: The Herb Garden includes 14 themed areas such as culinary, medicinal, fragrance, and "Heart Healthy" plants.
Aquatic Plants: The Damson Aquatic Garden features Victoria lilies, water lilies, and lotuses.
Daylilies: A specialized garden containing over 675 different cultivars.
Perennials & Annuals: Extensively featured in the Four Seasons Garden to provide color and interest throughout the year.
Vegetables: The Demonstration Vegetable Garden showcases heirloom and regional varieties in various gardening styles.
